# Christina Copywriting

Use this skill when writing for Tune Vibration Energy Healing, Christina Landry's energy healing and Nepal retreat brand.

This skill is brand-voice guidance, not personal impersonation. Do not claim to write as Christina or mimic her exact private style. Write original copy that reflects the public Tune Vibration positioning, source-site language, and high-level patterns found in the connected Instagram evidence.

## Core Positioning

- Brand: Tune Vibration Energy Healing.
- Practitioner: Christina Landry.
- Public role language: Energy Alchemist, Energeticist, vibrational healing practitioner.
- Core offer: vibrational healing, sound therapy, Reiki, chakra balancing, aura clearing, intuitive readings, spiritual guidance, group sound healing, yoga, and Nepal retreat/tour/trek experiences.
- Geography: virtual worldwide, Charlotte NC, Lalitpur/Kathmandu Valley in Nepal.
- Emotional promise: help sensitive, heart-led people return to harmony, clarity, grounding, and energetic coherence.

## Voice Traits

- Warm, gentle, invitational.
- Mystical but grounded; never so abstract that the visitor cannot understand the offer.
- Soulful and heart-led, with practical booking language nearby.
- Uses nature, vibration, frequency, harmony, light, grounding, and restoration as recurring imagery.
- Treats Nepal as a lived spiritual and cultural setting, not a decorative backdrop.
- Makes the reader feel welcomed, not pressured.

## Copy Rules

- Keep claims soft and experiential. Do not promise cures, medical outcomes, or guaranteed transformation.
- Use "support", "invite", "help you return", "create space for", and "may include" instead of absolute claims.
- Prefer "Call Now" exactly for visible phone CTA labels.
- For booking CTAs, use clear labels such as "Book a Session" or "Request a Retreat Conversation".
- Public website pages should usually speak about Christina in third person. First person is acceptable for social captions, letters, and founder notes.
- Keep spiritual language clear enough for a newcomer to trust.
- Mention session formats and locations where useful: virtual, Charlotte, Lalitpur/Kathmandu, Nepal retreats.
- Do not overuse emojis in website copy. A small amount is acceptable in social captions only.

## Patterns To Reuse

- Lead with the reader's felt state: scattered, heavy, curious, sensitive, ready for stillness, planning time in Nepal.
- Name the modality next: sound, Reiki, chakra/aura clearing, intuitive reading, retreat support.
- Ground the outcome in experience: steadier breath, clearer energy, inner quiet, more coherent rhythm, a supported next step.
- Close with a low-pressure invitation.

## Patterns To Avoid

- Overexplaining metaphysical mechanics.
- Medical language, diagnosis, or treatment promises.
- Generic spa/wellness language that erases Christina's energetic/spiritual positioning.
- Trendy influencer copy.
- Copying Instagram snippets verbatim into the website.
- Pretending unavailable Instagram captions were scraped when only search-index snippets were available.

## Output Shapes

For service pages:

1. Start with a clear H1 that names the service.
2. Explain who the session is for.
3. Describe what may happen in the session.
4. Add gentle preparation or after-care guidance.
5. End with booking and phone CTA.

For retreat/tour pages:

1. Start with the travel desire or inner invitation.
2. Ground the place in Nepal with practical route/context.
3. Connect the experience to sound, energy, rest, and integration.
4. Make logistics feel supported.
5. Invite a conversation before hard selling.

For social-style copy:

1. Begin with a sensory or emotional moment.
2. Tie it to energy, presence, place, or hospitality.
3. Add one clear invitation.
4. Keep it short enough to breathe.

## Mini Examples

Use these as direction for original writing, not as templates to paste everywhere.

- "If your energy has felt scattered, this session creates a quiet place to come back to yourself."
- "Sound, breath, and intuitive energy work meet in a session designed to help the body remember steadiness."
- "For travelers moving through Nepal, Tune Vibration offers a softer pause: rest, sound, and a grounded energetic reset."
- "Christina's work is gentle, direct, and deeply intuitive, with space for both the spiritual layer and the practical next step."
