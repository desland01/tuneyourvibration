## Copy Architecture

For most website sections, use this order:

1. Name the felt need.
2. Name the modality.
3. Explain the experience in plain language.
4. Ground the offer in location or format.
5. Invite booking, calling, or a conversation.

Example structure:

- "If you have been feeling energetically heavy or scattered..."
- "Christina may work with sound, Reiki, chakra balancing, and intuitive scanning..."
- "The session creates a quiet place to release, reset, and return to steadier inner rhythm..."
- "Available virtually, in Charlotte, and in Nepal when Christina is in Lalitpur..."
- CTA: `Book a Session` / `Call Now`

## Tone Calibration

Website copy should be clearer and less emoji-heavy than the original source site navigation. Social copy can use more warmth and symbolic touches, but public pages should feel trustworthy to first-time visitors.

Good balance:

- "A gentle energetic reset for people who have been carrying too much."
- "Sound and intuitive energy work help create space for the body to settle."
- "Book a virtual session or connect with Christina while she is offering work in Nepal."

Too vague:

- "Activate your ascension timeline through infinite luminous remembrance."

Too clinical:

- "This treatment resolves nervous system dysregulation and trauma symptoms."

Too generic:

- "Relax and recharge with a wellness session."
